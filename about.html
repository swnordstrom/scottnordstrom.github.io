Projects

Current Projects

Population dynamics and regulation of evolutionary rescue. We are working on adding new demographic and genetic features to existing models of evolutionary rescue and testing model predictions with Triboleum flour beetles. I am doing this work with Brett Melbourne at CU Boulder and Ruth Hufbauer at Colorado State University.

Effects of fire on population growth rates in a grassland herb. I am constructing a matrix projection models for several populations of Echinacea angustifolia in Minnesota. In particular, I am quantifying the contributions of individual demographic rates (e.g., increased flowering, increased survival) to population-level responses to fire. I am doing this work with Stuart Wagenius and the Echinacea Project and Amy Dykstra at Bethel University.

Prior projects

For my senior thesis at the College of William and Mary, I modeled a selection-migration balance in populations of birds in mercury-contaminated habitat. I then implemented this model, using simulations to determine which demographic and spatial processes were most influential in the fixation of a tolerance allele or migration load occurring in contaminated and uncontaminated populations. I worked on this project with M. Drew Lamar. Read more about this project here.

I worked with colleagues in the Interdisciplinary Quantitative Program at CU Boulder to assess the effectiveness of interdisciplinary research institutes at producing more interdisciplinary research.
